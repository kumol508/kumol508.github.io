My Way or the Highway (Code)
Unit 1 Elaborate
In the UK there are extensive Highway Codes that give instructions for both drivers and pedestrians to ensure order and safety on the road. Despite all of these rules in place, some people are not aware of the proper procedure and pedestrian crossings often do not function smoothly. Additionally, without the threat of immediate consequences, users of the crossing are more lax about following the rules.

This investigation also focuses on how the use of surveillance can create behavioural change. Using the King's Cross CCTV camera, I observed the zebra crossing on Goods Way and Granary Square and the way drivers and pedestrians interacted with each other. Secondary observation was done on site in a more obvious manner with a camera on a tripod so that both drivers and pedestrians were aware of the surveillance. Awareness of being watched and recorded primarily had an effect on the drivers at the crossing and pedestrians continued to violate various Highway Codes.
